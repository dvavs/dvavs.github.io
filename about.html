About Me

In a former life, I was on the path to become a professor of neuroscience and/or psychology. After 9 years pursuing that goal my priorities have changed, and in May, 2019 I threw myself headlong into the exciting world of development by joining the Full Stack Web Development Bootcamp at the University of Richmond (run by Trilogy Ed).


As you might expect of someone who was once interested in becoming a professor, I have an insatiable passion for learning. Since finishing the web development bootcamp, I've pointed that passion toward acquiring new skills in development (currently I'm working on Java and AWS). I aspire to continue pushing the breadth and depth of my skillset to become the best developer I can be. Particularly I am interested in expanding my knowledge as it relates to machine learning.


When I am not playing with code, you can find me hiking the Blue Ridge, reading academic papers (and sometimes writing my own!) or relaxing at home with my wife, two cats, and a good craft beer.
