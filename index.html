About Me

My name is Dylan Vavra. In 2014, I graduated from the University of Richmond with a Bachelor's in Psychology, and since then I have been working as a research assistant (in the very same department!). For as long as I can remember, my goal has been to attend graduate school, earn a Ph.D, and go on to be a professor. However, over the past few years I have gotten more of a holistic view of academia - the culture, the job market, the economic realities - and have concluded that the other goals I have for myself and my family are worth more to me. I've decided to venture on a new career path, so I've joined UR's Full Stack Web Development Bootcamp.

My background has helped me develop some skills that I think will come in handy as I transition into a different line of work. I am skilled in scientific writing, have exposure to a wide breadth of platforms (from Qualtrics, to Eyelink eyetracking systems, to Noldus behavioral observation software, to ArcGIS Pro and ArcGIS Online), and I am no stranger to hard work. I have spent considerable time debugging experiment applications and copyediting manuscripts for publication, and I pride myself on being very keenly attentive to detail. Relatedly, management of massive datasets and stimulus databases has helped me develop an accumen for digital organization. Finally, I have a thorough understanding of research design and data analysis.

When I am not learning new things in the world of web development, you can find me working on side research projects, hiking or rock climbing with my wife, or relaxing on the couch with my cats Herbie and Tiptoe. Occasionally I like to have a beer - even more so if I can do it in nature. My profile picture shows me doing just that, on a beach outside Dunedin, New Zealand.
